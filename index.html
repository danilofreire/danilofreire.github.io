Danilo Freire

Political Scientist · dfreire@lincoln.ac.uk

Greetings! My name is Danilo Freire and I am a Senior Lecturer in the School of Social and Political Sciences at the University of Lincoln. My academic credentials include a PhD in Political Economy from King's College London, an MA in International Relations from the Graduate Institute Geneva, alongside an MA in Political Science from the University of São Paulo. Prior to my current role, I served as a Postdoctoral Research Associate in The Political Theory Project at Brown University.

My scholarship addresses the challenges developing countries face in resolving collective action problems and eliminating political violence. Key themes that underpin my work include public service delivery, vertical and horizontal accountability, private governance, and state violence. Latin America is my principal region of focus, wherein I employ a range of methodological approaches and data sources, including survey and field experiments, machine learning algorithms, and administrative records.

My work has been featured in the American Journal of Political Science, the British Journal of Political Science, the Journal of Experimental Political Science, Latin American Research Review, Research & Politics, among others. I am also an active member of EGAP. To explore my recent projects, please follow this link.

You may access my full CV here, and my projects are available on GitHub. For any questions, inquiries, or potential collaborations, feel free to contact me at dfreire@lincoln.ac.uk or danilofreire@gmail.com. I look forward to connecting with you.
